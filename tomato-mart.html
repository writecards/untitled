Whenever the bass got too deep,
我们就从舞池逃出来，
eyes glitter-smeared, breath shallow—
踉踉跄跄地走进番茄超市。

Tomato Mart glowed like a memory that hadn’t happened yet.
亮亮的灯光、冰冷的饮品、
冰箱嗡嗡响着，好像派对还没结束。

We slurped cup noodles in silence.
沉默里泡面的声音像小小的雷鸣。
Salt, sugar, phosphorescence—
咸味、甜味、微弱的荧光—
our new communion.

The cashier never looked surprised.
收银员毫无反应。
She scanned us like usual.
我们只是上夜班的一部分。
